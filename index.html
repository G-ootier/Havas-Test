Key Message
Medication non-adherence is widely recognised as a major threat to patient outcomes. Yet healthcare professionals consistently lack the time, training, tools, and system support needed to address it effectively in routine care.

Improving adherence requires action on four fronts
Section 03
The
Path
Forward
Four pillars for systemic change
Pillar 01
Invest in Training
  • Communication and motivational interviewing skills
  • Adherence assessment tools and techniques
  • Managing medication side-effects
  • Simplifying treatment regimens
Pillar 02
Standardise Tools and Protocols
  • Clear, system-wide adherence protocols
  • Better digital integration into clinical workflows
  • Structured care pathways for long-term conditions
Pillar 03
Build Coordinated Care Teams
  • Dedicated adherence roles within multidisciplinary teams
  • Sustainable financing for adherence support functions
  • Strong managerial commitment and clinical leadership
Pillar 04
Put Patients at the Centre
  • Help patients understand their condition and treatment benefits
  • Address barriers specific to high-risk groups: young adults and men
  • Support long-term engagement beyond initial prescription
